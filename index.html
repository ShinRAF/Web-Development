Game Of The Year award goes to Red Dead Redemption 2 or God of War???!

The Game Awards 2018 has concluded and there were plenty of awards given out, but the awards were only given out to the best of the best in the gaming industry. But there some games which stood out from the rest, and these two are: Red Dead Redemption 2 and God of War. These two games were dominating and recieved and were nomitated for awards one after another.

Red Dead Redemption 2 recieved these awards:

  • Best Audio Design
  • Best Score/Music
  • Best Narritve
  • Best Score/Music
  • Best Performance(Voice Acting)

God of War recieved these awards:

  • Best Action/Adventure
  • Best Game Direction

After a long nerve wrecking wait awards getting thrown around, the community was so sure who would win the Game of the Year award. The clear canditate was Red Dead Redemption 2 because of it's record and numerios awards. But as the nomitates were getting shown for Game Of The Year, God of War and Red Dead Redemption 2 were nominated and the winner goes to....

Rejoice! God Of War by Santa Monica Studios have won the title of Game Of The Year. Againsts the odds, Kratos and Atreus showed the community that there are still hope for single player games, better luck next time Red Dead Redemption 2. With 2019 creeping around the corner, gamers are blessed with an amazing year.

One-Shot limited time demo??

Capcom's Resident Evil 2 Remake is having a time limited-demo released for PlayStation 4, Xbox One and PC via Steam on January 11 until January 31.

This demo is going to be special, this action-thriller demo can only be played once and after that one playthrough, the demo is going to lock itself and be unplayable and on top of that, the demo will have a time limit of 30 Minutues.

Capcom states:

"Aptly named the “1-Shot Demo,” players must take on the challenge of surviving the horrors of Raccoon City in just 30 minutes. If players complete the mission objective under 30 minutes, they can restart the mission until they use up their full time. If players succumb to the zombies during their 30 minutes, they can continue any number of times until the full 30 minutes have been reached.

The end of the demo reveals a brand new cinematic trailer exclusive to demo participants. While players cannot restart the playable demo after their time is up, there are no limitations on how many times the trailer can be viewed.

In the “1-Shot Demo,” players step into the boots of rookie police officer Leon S. Kennedy as he arrives at Raccoon City Police Station in the ultimate worst first day on the job. Leon must survive vicious zombies and solve puzzles to find safe passage out of the station. With an entire building of flesh-eating nightmares lurking between Leon and his escape while the clock ticks down, players need to be sure they’re killing more than just time."
